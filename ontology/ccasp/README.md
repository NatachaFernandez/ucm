# Care Coordination and Support Personnel (CCaSP)

## Overview

This directory contains the ontology artifacts supporting the Unified Care Model (UCM) representation of Care Coordination and Support Personnel (CCaSP).

CCaSP defines a canonical actor category used to normalize workforce roles involved in care coordination, navigation, referral management, care planning, outreach, and supportive services across healthcare, behavioral health, social care, and community settings.

The ontology is intended to support:

* UCM actor modeling
* Referral and care coordination workflows
* Implementation Guide (IG) development
* Terminology harmonization
* Cross-organizational interoperability

## Canonical Definition

Care Coordination and Support Personnel (CCaSP)

Individuals or workforce roles responsible for coordinating, navigating, facilitating, managing, monitoring, or supporting access to healthcare, behavioral health, social care, community resources, and related services, and who work with shared care plans or equivalent plans to promote continuity of care, reduce barriers, and help individuals achieve health and wellbeing goals across care settings and organizations.

Examples:

* Coordinate
* Navigate
* Assess
* Refer
* Monitor
* Document
* Advocate

### 05_Role_to_Capability

Role-to-function relationship matrix.

## Scope

Included:

* Workforce roles involved in coordination, navigation, support, and continuity activities.

Excluded:

* Clinical diagnosis
* Direct treatment authority
* Tools and technologies
* Service definitions

## Relationship to UCM

CCaSP represents the actor layer of the UCM and may interact with:

* Care Plans
* Referrals
* Assessments
* Supportive Services
* Care Teams
