# Care Plan Measurement

## Purpose

This document defines measurement concepts for Care Plans within the Unified Care Model (UCM).

Measurement supports understanding current state, monitoring progress, evaluating effectiveness, informing decisions, supporting accountability, and adapting Care Plans over time.

Measurement enables Care Plans to function as living coordination constructs rather than static planning artifacts.

This document is implementation independent.

---

# Principles

Care Plan measurement is:

* person-centered
* goal-oriented
* measurable
* longitudinal
* episodic when appropriate
* evidence informed
* outcome aware
* adaptive
* interoperable
* transparent

Measurement should support action and learning rather than collection for its own sake.

# Core Measurement Components

## Baseline

Represents the initial state against which future progress may be evaluated.

Examples:

* initial assessment
* intake state
* starting measures
* eligibility status

Baseline may change over time.

---

## Indicator

Represents a measurable signal used to evaluate status or change.

Examples:

* clinical indicator
* social indicator
* engagement indicator
* operational indicator
* access indicator

Indicators support interpretation.

---

## Assessment

Represents evaluation activities that generate measurement inputs.

Examples:

* needs assessment
* structured evaluation
* reassessment
* screening
* instrument administration

Assessments may repeat.

---

## Goal

Represents intended outcomes.

Goals may include:

* preventive goals
* access goals
* wellbeing goals
* intervention goals
* functional goals

Goals define success.

---

## Measure

Represents specific measurable values.

Examples:

* scores
* counts
* percentages
* milestones
* completion status
* durations
* observations

Measures may be quantitative or qualitative.

---

## Observation

Represents collected evidence.

Examples:

* self-report
* participant observation
* instrument output
* service completion
* status updates

Observations inform evaluation.

---

## Intervention Measurement

Represents evaluation of actions taken.

Examples:

* intervention adherence
* intervention completion
* intervention response
* intervention effectiveness

Interventions should be measurable where appropriate.

---

## Progress

Represents movement over time.

Progress may include:

* milestone achievement
* trajectory
* improvement
* maintenance
* regression

Progress informs adaptation.

---

## Outcome

Represents observed effects.

Examples:

* improved access
* reduced barriers
* improved wellbeing
* achieved goals
* sustained outcomes

Outcomes may be intended or unintended.

---

## Evaluation

Represents interpretation of measured information.

Examples:

* effectiveness evaluation
* trend analysis
* comparison
* review

Evaluation supports decisions.

---

## Target

Represents desired future values.

Examples:

* target score
* expected completion
* intended outcome

Targets may evolve.

---

## Threshold

Represents conditions that trigger action.

Examples:

* risk threshold
* eligibility threshold
* escalation threshold
* intervention threshold

Thresholds support responsiveness.

---

## Review

Represents formal reassessment cycles.

Examples:

* scheduled review
* milestone review
* triggered review

Review may result in revision.

---

## Adjustment

Represents modification based on measurement.

Examples:

* intervention updates
* revised goals
* new services
* changed coordination

Measurement should lead to action when appropriate.

---

## Learning

Represents accumulated insight.

Examples:

* what worked
* what did not work
* emerging patterns
* improved planning approaches

Learning enables continuous improvement.

* observations

* service records

# Structured Instruments

Measurement may be informed by structured instruments.

Examples include:

* needs assessments
* social risk screening
* behavioral instruments
* outcome instruments
* functional instruments
* eligibility tools

Instruments support but do not replace judgment.

---

# Temporal Patterns

## Point-in-Time

Single observation.

Examples:

* intake
* enrollment
* discharge

---

## Episodic

Measured across a defined period.

Examples:

* intervention episode
* transition period

---

## Longitudinal

Measured continuously across time.

Examples:

* chronic management
* preventive planning
* sustained coordination

Measurement models may coexist.
