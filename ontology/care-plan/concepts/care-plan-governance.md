# Care Plan Governance

## Purpose

This document defines governance concepts for Care Plans within the Unified Care Model (UCM).

Governance establishes how Care Plans are created, maintained, contributed to, reviewed, approved, shared, versioned, protected, and acted upon across participants, organizations, programs, and systems.

Care Plan governance enables trust, continuity, accountability, interoperability, and shared decision making while recognizing that ownership and authority may be distributed.

This document is implementation independent.

---

# Principles

Care Plan governance is:

* person-centered
* collaborative
* transparent
* accountable
* traceable
* adaptive
* interoperable
* role aware
* policy aware
* implementation independent

Governance supports stewardship rather than exclusive ownership.

---

# Governance Objectives

Governance exists to ensure:

* clear accountability
* coordinated decision making
* continuity across transitions
* appropriate authority
* safe collaboration
* controlled change management
* historical preservation
* measurement and oversight
* equitable participation

# Governance Components

## Stewardship

Represents responsibility for maintaining continuity, integrity, and usability of the Care Plan.

Stewards may:

* maintain the plan
* coordinate updates
* facilitate participation
* monitor completion
* preserve continuity

Stewardship does not imply ownership.

Examples:

* care coordinator
* care team
* community organization
* individual
* delegated participant

---

## Ownership

Represents responsibility and accountability for the Care Plan.

Ownership may include:

* individual ownership
* shared ownership
* organizational ownership
* delegated ownership

Ownership may change over time.

Multiple owners may coexist.

---

## Participation

Defines who may:

* view
* contribute
* propose changes
* approve
* execute
* monitor
* evaluate

Participation may vary by context.

---

## Authority

Defines who may make decisions.

Examples:

* approve changes
* initiate interventions
* assign activities
* authorize transitions
* manage governance rules

Authority may be:

* advisory
* delegated
* operational
* supervisory
* administrative

Authority is context dependent.

---

## Decision Rights

Defines allowable governance actions.

Examples:

* create
* revise
* approve
* reject
* escalate
* close
* reopen

Decision rights may differ by participant.

---

## Contribution

Represents permitted participation in Care Plan development.

Contribution may include:

* goals
* assessments
* interventions
* evidence
* measurements
* comments
* updates

Contribution rights may vary.

---

## Approval

Represents formal acceptance.

Approval may occur by:

* individual
* caregiver
* care team
* organization
* payer
* regulatory process

Approval may be optional.

---

## Review

Represents structured evaluation cycles.

Examples:

* scheduled review
* milestone review
* triggered review
* outcome review

Reviews may lead to revision.

---

## Consent

Represents permissions governing participation and information sharing.

Examples:

* consent to participate
* consent to exchange
* consent to share
* delegated consent

Consent requirements vary.

---

## Access

Defines who may access:

* plans
* goals
* assessments
* interventions
* outcomes
* history

Access may depend on:

* role
* relationship
* policy
* consent
* jurisdiction

---

## Versioning

Represents management of Care Plan evolution.

Version governance supports:

* revision history
* effective periods
* traceability
* rollback
* comparison

Historical integrity should be preserved.

---

## Audit

Represents accountability mechanisms.

Audit examples:

* change history
* contributor attribution
* approvals
* access logs
* governance actions

Audit supports trust.

---

## Delegation

Represents transfer of responsibility.

Examples:

* delegated coordination
* proxy participation
* delegated approvals

Delegation may be temporary.

---

## Transition Governance

Defines governance during movement across contexts.

Examples:

* discharge
* referral completion
* transfer
* enrollment change
* provider transition

Transitions should preserve continuity.

---

## Closure Governance

Defines conditions for concluding active governance.

Examples:

* goals achieved
* withdrawal
* inactivity
* completed episode
* transition complete

Closure does not imply deletion.

# Governance Patterns

## Individual-Led

Individual acts as primary steward.

Examples:

* wellness plans
* preventive planning

---

## Team-Based

Multiple participants share governance.

Examples:

* interdisciplinary planning

---

## Program-Led

Program governs lifecycle.

Examples:

* care management
* eligibility coordination

---

## Organization-Led

Organization manages governance.

Examples:

* accountable care models

---

## Distributed Governance

Authority distributed across participants.

Examples:

* community coordination
