# Care Plan Concepts

## Purpose

This folder contains conceptual definitions and semantic guidance for the Care Plan domain within the Unified Care Model (UCM).

These documents describe what a Care Plan is, how it behaves, what components it contains, how it evolves over time, and how it supports coordination, planning, interventions, measurement, and interoperability.

The Concepts folder serves as the authoritative conceptual layer for the Care Plan ontology and complements structured ontology artifacts, diagrams, and exported machine-readable representations.

---

# Scope

The Care Plan domain supports:

* interdisciplinary care management and coordination
* care planning and plan execution
* eligibility determination and enrollment
* assessment and reassessment
* evidence-based interventions
* service planning and navigation
* goal management
* outcome measurement
* longitudinal and episodic coordination
* continuity across settings and domains

Care Plans are not limited to clinical care and may apply across healthcare, behavioral health, social care, public health, community services, wellness, prevention, and related contexts.

---

# Design Principles

Care Plans within UCM are modeled as:

* living and evolving
* person-centered
* goal-oriented
* measurable
* interoperable
* interdisciplinary
* collaborative
* longitudinal and episodic
* implementation independent

A Care Plan is not treated as a static document.

A Care Plan may exist as:

* shared planning artifact
* structured record
* coordination workflow
* interoperable resource
* decision support mechanism
* longitudinal state representation
